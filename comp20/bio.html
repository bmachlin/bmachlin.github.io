I was hiking through the mountains on a cool but sunny day when I happened upon a strange habitat. It was carved into the base of a towering, ancient tree. The opening was not much bigger than a small gourd or perhaps three large mushrooms, and the surrounding grass was worn from heavy use. I continued to examine the dwelling; I had never seen anything like it in all my travels.

"You'd better not stay long, fella. That thing doesn't seem to like visitors," a small voice chimed from behind me.

I turned to face the voice and was greeted by a rabbit, its body halfway out of its rabbit hole. "Oh hello, Mr. Rabbit. Why is that?"

"My name's Henry," the rabbit named Henry replied, slightly iritated. He continued, "I've never seem what lives in there but nothing that's gone in has ever come out."

"Don't worry, Henry. My friends aren't far behind me, they'll have my back." His nosed continued twitching like a rabbit's nose does. Then he disappeared silently into the ground.

I turned to face the hole again. "He's right, you know," the tree rumbled. I couldn't be sure if it was the tree speaking to me or a mysterious creature that dwelt inside it.

"Though I have learned much over the many years I've occupied this hillside, I lack the ability to see inside myself. I fear what lies in my trunk will be my demise."

"Is it hurting you?"

"While I can survive without the flesh it has seized, it has nearly severed my connection to the forest. A tree like me cannot survive alone." I could feel the ground vibrate slightly in tune with the voice and could hear the age in its tone. I was undoubtedly speaking with the tree itself.

"I'm sorry to hear that." I mused for a moment then sat in front of the opening.

I couldn't see very far inside so I cautiously moved closer to get a better look. Yet, I still could not see much. Curiosity gripped me like a constricting vine. I placed my backpack next to the tree, laid down and stuck my face into the opening.

"Oh my. It's actually quite spacious in here," I announced to the tree, unsure of whether it could hear me from the inside.

I slowly crawled my way all the way in to the cavernous arboreal chamber and began my wait.

I had seen the other hikers on the same trail as I took. They were sure to pass by soon and spot the bait.
