Here's where I'll jot down some general life lessons and related thoughts - all of which I'm sure to one day laugh at. In the meantime, I hope both writing and reading these musings will help to remind me of their truth (however obscured it is by shoddy writing :P).

Amend mistakes ASAP

March 25, 2021

We all fuck up, a lot. I fuck up constantly. Whether it's a bug in some code, or being mean, or slacking on some responsibility, the best practice is to address thee error as soon as possible. This means apologizing as soon as you realize your mistake. This means not letting things fester. This means admitting you're wrong.

When it's an interpersonal issue, the two outcomes of not addressing a mistake are much worse than overzealously copping to it immediately. 1- you'll sweat over what the other person(s) think about you and 2- they might come to resent you even if they don't show it. Apologize immediately and without shame; at the very least they'll know you didn't mean to do whatever heinous act it was. Related - it's ok to send an email/text with a mistake and follow up with a correction.

When it's a personal issue, it's much easier to rationalize away. I've been doing this since my first homework assignment. But now that I'm not in school and have evermore responsiblities to tend to, any procrastination comes back to bite me in a more personal, directly affecting way. 100% of the time, I always wish I'd done things sooner, right when I realized it was an issue.

Related thoughts

  • Nip small things in the bud as soon as they pop up.
  • Getting things done quickly also makes you feel good and productive even if the task is small.
  • People forget about things you do very quickly - apologizing leaves a better taste than an unacknolwedged mistake.
  • People who admit when they're wrong are more trustworthy IMO.

Know when to drop $$$. Not every purchase has to be a good deal.

March 25, 2021

For a long time I was super frugal. I relied on holiday gifts and saving up allowance/extra lunch money as an 'income'. Now that I have a real big boy job I also have some real big boy money. It's been a slow process but I'm learning just how worth it it is to splurge on the right things. Convenience, unique opportunities/experiences, good food, long term items, and life affirming items are all worth the X% extra they always seems to cost. Conversely, it's probably for the better that certain less wholesome items give me pause - e.g. new video games, fancy phone, drinks at clubs.

Related thoughts

  • Tipping well is always a good idea.
  • Spending money on others is almost always a good idea.
  • When you have limited resources - e.g. in-game currency, some # of candies left - you don't have to ration it to the extreme. A lot of the time there's no reward for saving a lot of it for the end. Consume as you please, but savor it.
